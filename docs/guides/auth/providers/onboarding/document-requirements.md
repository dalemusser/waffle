# Privacy Policy & Terms of Service Requirements

*What your institution needs to know to create or approve legal documents for K-12 authentication provider registration.*

---

## Purpose

This document outlines the requirements for Privacy Policy and Terms of Service documents needed to register with K-12 Single Sign-On (SSO) providers. It is intended for:

- **Project Directors** — To understand what documents are needed
- **Legal Counsel** — To draft or review documents that meet provider requirements
- **Compliance Officers** — To ensure FERPA/COPPA requirements are addressed

---

## Document Overview

### What Providers Require

| Provider | Privacy Policy | Terms of Service |
|----------|:--------------:|:----------------:|
| Google | **Required** | Recommended |
| Clever | **Required** | Optional |
| ClassLink | **Required** | Optional |
| Microsoft | **Required** | Recommended |
| Schoology | **Required** | Optional |

### Document Format Options

Providers accept either:

1. **Combined Document** — Single "Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy" document
2. **Separate Documents** — Individual Privacy Policy and Terms of Service documents

Both approaches are acceptable. Choose based on your institution's preference and existing document standards.

### Publication Requirements

- Documents must be publicly accessible via URL (e.g., `https://yourapp.example.com/privacy`)
- Documents should be accessible without login
- URLs are provided during provider registration

## Required Content: Privacy Policy

The Privacy Policy must address the following areas to satisfy all K-12 authentication providers.

### Data Collection

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Types of data collected** | Clearly state what personal information is collected (name, email address, user role, school affiliation) |
| **Source of data** | Explain that data comes from the SSO provider (Google, Clever, etc.) during authentication |
| **Authentication-only clarification** | If applicable, explicitly state that you only collect data for authentication—not grades, assignments, or roster data |

**Example language:**
> "When you sign in using [Google/Clever/ClassLink/Microsoft/Schoology], we receive your name, email address, and role (student or teacher) from that service. We do not request or receive grades, assignments, course enrollments, or other educational records."

### Data Usage

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Purpose of collection** | Explain why each piece of data is collected and how it is used |
| **User-facing features only** | For Google specifically: state that data is only used for user-facing features, not sold or used for advertising |

**Example language:**
> "We use your name and email address to create your account and identify you within the application. We use your role (student/teacher) to provide appropriate access to features."

### Data Sharing

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Third-party sharing policy** | State whether data is shared with third parties |
| **No selling of data** | Explicitly state that user data is not sold |
| **Legal requirements** | Note that data may be disclosed if required by law |
| **Service providers** | If you use third-party services (hosting, analytics), disclose this |

**Example language:**
> "We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for their marketing purposes. We may share data with service providers who assist in operating our application (such as cloud hosting), and we may disclose information if required by law."

### Data Retention & Deletion

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Retention period** | State how long data is kept |
| **Deletion process** | Explain how users can request data deletion |
| **School/district deletion requests** | For K-12: explain how schools can request deletion of their users' data |
| **Contact for deletion requests** | Provide contact information for deletion requests |

**Example language:**
> "We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide services. You may request deletion of your data at any time by contacting [email]. Schools may request deletion of all data associated with their students and staff."

### Data Security

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Security measures** | Describe how data is protected (encryption, access controls, etc.) |
| **No guarantee disclaimer** | Note that no method is 100% secure |

**Example language:**
> "We implement industry-standard security measures including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and regular security reviews. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security."

### Children's Privacy (COPPA)

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **COPPA compliance statement** | Required for applications used by children under 13 |
| **Parental consent mechanism** | Explain how parental consent is obtained (typically through school authorization) |
| **School as agent** | Clarify that schools act as agents for parental consent under FERPA |

**Example language:**
> "Our application is designed for use in K-12 schools. We rely on schools to obtain appropriate consent for students under 13 as permitted under COPPA. Schools act as agents of parents for the purposes of consenting to the collection of student personal information in the educational context."

### Educational Records (FERPA)

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **FERPA compliance statement** | Required for applications handling student data |
| **School official exception** | If applicable, state that you operate under the "school official" exception |
| **Educational purpose** | Explain the legitimate educational interest |

**Example language:**
> "We comply with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). We operate under the 'school official' exception, providing services that the school would otherwise perform itself. We use student information solely for the educational purposes for which we were engaged."

### User Rights

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Access rights** | Explain how users can access their data |
| **Correction rights** | Explain how users can correct inaccurate data |
| **Deletion rights** | Explain how users can request deletion |
| **Complaint process** | Provide contact information for privacy concerns |

**Example language:**
> "You have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal information. To exercise these rights, contact us at [email]. Parents may review their child's information by contacting their school."

### Contact Information

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Privacy contact** | Email address for privacy questions |
| **Organization address** | Physical mailing address |
| **Response timeframe** | Optional but recommended: state how quickly you respond to inquiries |

## Required Content: Terms of Service

Terms of Service (also called Terms of Use or Terms & Conditions) are recommended by Google and Microsoft. They protect both you and users by establishing the rules of engagement.

### Acceptance of Terms

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Agreement to terms** | State that using the service constitutes acceptance |
| **Age/authority requirements** | Note that users must have authority to accept (or school accepts on behalf of students) |

### Acceptable Use

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Permitted uses** | Describe what the application is for |
| **Prohibited conduct** | List activities that are not allowed |
| **Account responsibility** | Users are responsible for their account security |

### Intellectual Property

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | State who owns the application content and trademarks |
| **User content** | If users create content, clarify ownership and licenses |
| **Restrictions** | Prohibit unauthorized copying or modification |

### Disclaimers & Limitations

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **"As is" disclaimer** | Service is provided without warranties |
| **Limitation of liability** | Limit liability for damages |
| **Jurisdictional variations** | Note that some jurisdictions don't allow certain limitations |

### Termination

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Right to terminate** | You may terminate access for violations |
| **Effect of termination** | What happens to user data upon termination |

### Governing Law

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Jurisdiction** | Which state/country's laws govern |
| **Dispute resolution** | Where disputes will be resolved |

### Changes to Terms

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Right to modify** | You may update terms |
| **Notice of changes** | How users will be notified |
| **Continued use = acceptance** | Using service after changes means acceptance |

## Provider-Specific Requirements

### Google

- Requires compliance with [Google API Services User Data Policy](https://developers.google.com/terms/api-services-user-data-policy)
- Must state that data obtained through Google Login is only used to provide user-facing features
- Privacy policy URL is required during OAuth consent screen configuration
- Terms of Service URL is optional but recommended

### Clever

- Strong emphasis on student data privacy
- Membership in [Student Data Privacy Consortium (SDPC)](https://privacy.a4l.org/) can expedite school approvals
- Certification process reviews your privacy practices
- Privacy policy URL required during registration

### ClassLink

- Privacy policy URL required during partner registration
- Emphasis on "interoperability shouldn't cost a dime" — no hidden data monetization
- SDPC membership helpful but not required

### Microsoft

- Requires compliance with [Microsoft Identity Platform policies](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/identity-platform/)
- Privacy policy and Terms of Service URLs displayed on consent screen
- Multi-tenant apps need clear disclosure of data handling

### Schoology

- Privacy policy URL required for App Center listing
- Developer Terms of Use must be accepted
- OAuth 1.0a protocol (older standard, but same privacy requirements)

## Special Considerations for Universities/Research Institutions

If this application is part of a research project:

| Consideration | Recommendation |
|---------------|----------------|
| **IRB approval** | If collecting data for research, mention IRB approval in privacy policy |
| **Research exemptions** | FERPA has research exemptions—consult with your IRB and legal counsel |
| **Data use for research** | Clearly disclose if data will be used for research purposes |
| **De-identification** | If publishing research, explain how data will be de-identified |
| **Funding disclosure** | Consider disclosing funding source (e.g., "funded by Department of Education") |

## Questions to Answer Before Drafting

Provide these answers to whoever is drafting the documents:

1. **What data do you collect?** (name, email, role — anything else?)
2. **How is data used?** (authentication only, or also for analytics, research, etc.?)
3. **Do you use any third-party services?** (cloud hosting, analytics, error tracking?)
4. **How long do you keep data?** (duration or "until deletion requested"?)
5. **Is this for research?** (if so, is there IRB approval?)
6. **Who should users contact for privacy questions?** (email, phone, address)
7. **What state/country law governs?** (typically where your institution is located)
8. **Do you use cookies or tracking?** (analytics, session cookies, etc.)
